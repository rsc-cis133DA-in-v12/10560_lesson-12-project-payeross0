Our Commitment to Accessibility

We are committed in ensuring our website is as accessible and easy-to-use as possible for everyone.

Web Users and Web Accessibility

Disability Description Common Barriers Accessibility Principles to Apply
Auditory Mild or moderate hearing loss in one or both ears, or complete and total deafness Audio content without captions or transcripts, or without an option to adjust captions; difficult to read text; lack of sign language; voice only interactions Perceivable, Operable
Cognitive and Neurological Neurodiversity and Neurological disorders and/or behavioral and mental health disorders, affecting any part of the nervous system as well as impacting how someone hears, moves, sees, speaks, or understands information Complex page layouts; difficult or unusual words; lack of illustrations; moving or blink content Perceivable, Understandable, Operable, Robust
Physical Weakness and limitations of muscular control, limitations of sensation, joint disorders, missing limbs, or pain that impedes movement No full keyboard support; insufficient time limits; missing visual or navigational aids; overly complicated page functions Perceivable, Operable, Robust
Speech Difficultly producing speech that is recognizable by others or voice detection software web services relying on using voice only; websites only using phone numbers to communicate Operable, Robust
Visual Mild or moderate vision loss in one or both eyes to complete blindness, lack of sensitivity to certain colors,including variations in perception of color and brightness Image, controls, structures with no alternative text; fixed layouts with no option to resize; non-visual cues or aids; insufficient contrast between background and foreground colors; unsupported custom color combinations Perceivable, Understandable, Operable, Robust
